About Us

The Oceans are Suffering

Save Our Oceans is a Website dedicated to raise awareness on the current state of our oceans, and if you have been up-to-date with the news, it's not so good, and it's because of us.

People have assumed our vast ocean to be boundless and immune to human impacts for centuries. Researchers have only recently come to understand the devastating effects that we have already had on our oceans.

We are witnessing a pollution crisis around the world with such an incredible amount of plastic waste taking over our oceans. Trash is eaten by marine life and has entered all levels of the food chain — even ending up in the seafood on our plates.

There is currently very little protection for our threatened marine life and dwindling fish stocks — with less than 2% of our oceans set aside as marine reserves, it has become all too easy for our natural resources to be exploited from lack of protection.

It's now time to act. The threats to the health of our oceans posed by plastic pollution and overfishing are clear—we face irreversible damage and the collapse of some of the world's most important food sources without adequate protections and corporate responsibility.

Did You Know?

80% of marine pollution comes from land-based sources.

The 5 most common items found in coastal cleanups around the world are all single-use plastics. cigarette butts, food wrappers, plastic beverage bottles, plastic bottle caps, straws and drink stirrers.

Ocean pollutions kills more than one million sea birds each year.

Over 100,000 marine animals die every year from plastic entanglement and ingestion.

How To Help

We Can Do Something
About It

While many of these problems would include government and corporate leadership, there are plenty of steps you can take that will make a big difference to the ocean.

1. Carbon footprint & energy consumption reduction

Reduce the effects of climate change on the environment by leaving the car at home and being mindful of your home and work energy use. To get started today, you can do a few things: switch to compact fluorescent bulbs, take the stairs, bundle up or use a fan to avoid over-setting your thermostat.

2. Avoid Plastic Products

Plastics that end up as ocean debris each year contribute to habitat destruction and interfere with and kill tens of thousands of marine animals. Bring a reusable water bottle to limit your impact, store food in non-disposable containers, bring your own cloth tote or other reusable bag when you shop, and recycle whenever possible.

3. Help Take Care of the Beach

Whether on the beach you enjoy scuba diving, surfing or relaxing, always clean up after yourself. Discover and enjoy the ocean without messing with animals or damaging coral and rocks. Go further by encouraging others to respect the marine environment or to take part in local beach clean-ups.

4. Influence Change in Your Community

Research public officials ' ocean policies before you vote or contact your local officials to let them know that you are supporting marine conservation projects. Consider patronising restaurants and grocery stores that only offer sustainable seafood, and if you spot a threatened species on the menu or on the seafood counter, discuss your concerns.

5. Educate Yourself About Oceans & Marine Life

The ocean and its inhabitants are connected to all life on Earth. The more you learn about the problems this vital system faces, the more you want to help ensure its health — then share that knowledge in order to educate and inspire others.
